VISIT LOCATION

The red castle

It is considered one of the oldest and largest Libyan museums, and it is practically divided into four levels “museums” that display the heritage and history of Libya through the periods that include the civilizations of the Libyan tribes, the Greeks, the Phoenicians, the Romans, and the Arab eras.

Marcus Aurelius Arch

It is located in the city of Tripoli, the capital of Libya. It is an arch to commemorate the Roman Emperor Marcus Aurelius, who ruled between the years (161-180).

qasr al khold ( mole palace)

It is the palace that was the royal residence of Libya between the years 1951-1964, when King Idris I took it as an official residence between 1951-1964.

Maydan al shohada ( Martyrs' Square )

It is located next to the Saraya, founded in the twenties of the twentieth century, when it bore the name "Italy Square". After Libya's independence in 1951, it became called "Independence Square", then changed to "Martyrs' Square" and then to "Green Square", to return to the name "Martyrs' Square" in The last years of Gaddafi's rule, and the last name was installed after the February 17 revolution.

the old city

It is located in Tripoli, the capital of Libya. It is considered the ancient center of the city of Tripoli, which overlooks the Mediterranean Sea. It is surrounded by a wall and contains a number of shops and cafes. The old city also contains a large number of archaeological and historical buildings .It was founded by the Phoenicians between the fifth and seventh centuries BC

Gurgi Mosque

One of the oldest mosques in the Libyan capital, Tripoli. and a part of the ancient buildings and monuments in the old city(al Medina). The mosque attracts a large number of tourists.

Algeria Square Mosque

Algeria Square Mosque or Gamal Abdel Nasser Mosque, was previously the Cathedral of Tripoli, a Roman Catholic church founded in the twenties of the twentieth century, during the era of the Italian occupation and was officially opened in 1928. The cathedral was not affiliated with the Italian government, but it belonged to the Vatican State and is located in a square previously known as “Cathedral Square” now “Algiers Square” in the center of the capital.

Youssef Gramali's house

Hawsh al-Qara Manli or the house of al-Qara Manli is a house closer to a palace dating back to the early nineteenth century, built by Yusuf al-Qara Manli.The Qara Manli family ruled Tripoli Libya from the second half of the eighteenth century until the first third of the nineteenth century. The house is now open to visitors, offering a presentation of its contents and the lifestyle of its former occupants.

Church of Virgin Mary

This church is located in the Old City, within the borders of Bab Al-Bahr area in Tripoli, in the square named after Maryam Square. The age of this church exceeds 400 years, as construction of the church began in 1615 AD, and it was expanded and maintained in the years 1703 and 1829.

Tripoli Tower

Tripoli Tower is a tower and skyscraper in Tripoli, Libya. It is located in the city center of Tripoli near the central business district. The building was opened in 2003. The tower houses retail outlets and hotel apartments. There are four floors of basement parking

The Clock Tower

in Houmt al-Baladiya in the Old City of Tripoli, Libya. The tower is surmounted by a clock that contains a bell that rings each at the top of the clock. It is considered one of the archaeological monuments in the city of Tripoli. The governor at the time, Ali Reda Pasha, ordered its construction, and construction continued during the years (1866-1870). The tower overlooks The Red Serail and the headquarters of the Central Bank of Libya.
